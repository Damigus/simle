About Stardust

Stardust is an interdisciplinary project led by students from the Gdańsk University of Technology, focused on launching stratospheric balloon experiments in the REXUS/BEXUS and Global Space Balloon Challenge competitions.

The idea for stratospheric balloon missions came to us in 2015. After successful initial missions aimed at reaching the stratosphere and testing the solutions we were developing, we were ready to carry out more complex experiments. We invited young scientists from the Biotechnology Student Circle at Gdańsk University of Technology to collaborate on microbiological missions.

Our mission continues with experiments on the microbial flora of the stratosphere, as well as studying the relationship between cosmic radiation and the risk of cancer, a subject which could inspire future researchers.

Our Journey: The Evolution of Stardust

Stardust began as an ambitious student initiative driven by a passion for aerospace engineering and scientific exploration. From the early stages of research and development to the actual launches of stratospheric balloons, the project has grown steadily. Over time, we have expanded our work to include microbiological research in collaboration with the Biotechnology Student Circle.

Stardust Development Timeline

2015

Stardust project established. First missions focused on reaching the stratosphere and testing new solutions.

2017

Collaboration with the Biotechnology Student Circle to launch microbiological experiments and sample collection from the stratosphere.

2019

Successful participation in the REXUS/BEXUS competition, launching experiments related to microbiology and cosmic radiation.

2020

Participation in a stratospheric mission from Esrange Space Center in Sweden, involving microbiological and radiation experiments.

2023

Ongoing development of biological experiments and the creation of a vacuum chamber prototype for simulating stratospheric conditions.
