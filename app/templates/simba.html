About SimBa

SimBa is our pioneering sounding rocket program at the SimLE Scientific Association, pushing the boundaries of aerospace engineering through student innovation.

Our team designs, manufactures, and launches experimental rockets that serve as platforms for scientific research, technological development, and educational outreach.

By drawing together students from mechanical engineering, electronics, computer science, and materials science, SimBa embodies the interdisciplinary approach that defines SimLE's vision.

Each rocket iteration represents our commitment to continuous improvement, incorporating cutting-edge components, materials, and design strategies to achieve ever more ambitious mission objectives.

Our Journey: The Evolution of SimBa

SimBa began as a bold student initiative driven by passion for aerospace engineering and scientific exploration. What started as sketches and calculations has evolved into a comprehensive rocket development program with multiple successful launches and innovative designs. Our team has grown from a handful of enthusiasts to a diverse group of specialists working across multiple engineering disciplines.

SimBa Development Timeline

2017

Establishment of the SimBa project and the start of designing the first rocket, R1. Its main feature was the ability to actively control its trajectory using fins.

2018

R2 prototype completed and first static engine tests conducted. The team expanded with new members bringing expertise in composite materials and electronics.

2019

R3 rocket built with improved avionics system and successful test flight reaching 2.5km altitude. First international competition participation.

2020

R4 rocket designed with advanced propulsion system. Virtual development continued despite pandemic restrictions.

2023

R6 rocket built and showcased at the Spaceport America Cup competition, reaching a new altitude record for the team.
