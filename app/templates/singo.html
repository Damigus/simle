About Singo

Singo is our pioneering Arctic drone program at SimLE, aiming to revolutionize data collection from polar regions through innovative aerial technology.

The project addresses the challenges of glacier mass balance measurement, offering a safer and more efficient alternative to current methods. Our drones, equipped with radar and sonar, gather precise snow cover height data from glaciers, improving the accuracy of sea level rise models.

Singo combines advanced sensor technology with cutting-edge drone systems to provide crucial data for climate change prediction and adaptation strategies. This is our contribution to a better understanding of climate dynamics and its impact on global sea levels.

Our Journey: The Evolution of Singo

Singo began as a response to the critical need for more accurate and accessible data from polar regions. The project was developed by a team of engineers and scientists with a vision to revolutionize how snow cover is measured, particularly on glaciers. From the early stages of conceptual design to the first successful drone flights in Arctic conditions, Singo represents the cutting-edge of drone-based environmental data collection.

Singo Development Timeline

2021

Initiation of the Singo project with the goal of developing an aerial drone system for snow cover measurement on glaciers in polar regions.

2022

Completion of the first prototype drone equipped with radar and sonar sensors. Initial tests conducted in subarctic conditions.

2023

Successful field tests in the Arctic. Data gathered from glaciers significantly improved the accuracy of sea level rise projections.

2024

Ongoing development of miniaturized sonar technology to reduce drone weight. Project continues to gather invaluable data from polar regions.
