About Stellar

Stellar is our innovative lunar rover program at LunAR, aiming to explore and map the lunar surface using advanced robotics and autonomous systems.

The project tackles the challenges of lunar exploration, offering a safer and more efficient method of surface exploration on the Moon. Our rovers, equipped with advanced sensors and mobility systems, will assist in the search for water ice and valuable minerals, enhancing the potential for future lunar habitation.

Stellar combines cutting-edge robotics with autonomous navigation to support NASA’s Artemis mission and future human missions to the Moon.

Our Journey: The Evolution of Stellar

Stellar began as a response to the growing need for reliable and efficient lunar exploration vehicles. The project was developed by a team of roboticists, engineers, and scientists with a shared vision to create a rover capable of traversing the Moon's harsh environment and contributing to the scientific understanding of the lunar surface. From the initial design phase to the successful development of the first lunar rover, Stellar is advancing the frontier of space exploration.

Stellar Development Timeline

2023

Initiation of the Stellar project with the goal of designing and building a lunar rover for surface exploration in support of future lunar missions.

2024

Completion of the first prototype lunar rover. Successful testing of mobility and sensor systems in simulated lunar environments.

2025

First field tests of the Stellar rover on simulated lunar terrain. Data gathered will contribute to the planning of future lunar missions.

2026

Completion of second-generation rover with enhanced autonomy and advanced scientific instruments. Project continues to contribute to lunar exploration.
